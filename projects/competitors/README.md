# Competitors

## Purpose
This folder contains competitive intelligence - who you compete with and how you win/lose against them.

## What Goes Here

### Competitive Profiles
Detailed profiles of your direct and indirect competitors:
- Company overview
- Product capabilities
- Pricing and packaging
- Target market and positioning
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Win/loss themes

### Market Analysis
- Market map and segmentation
- Category trends
- Competitive landscape shifts
- New entrants to watch

### Battle Cards
Quick-reference guides for competitive situations:
- Head-to-head comparisons
- Differentiation points
- Objection handling
- Proof points

## File Format

Save competitive intel as markdown files:
- `competitor-[name].md` - Individual competitor profiles
- `competitors.md` - Consolidated competitive overview
- `battle-cards/` - Quick-reference comparison sheets
- `win-loss-analysis.md` - Patterns from won and lost deals

## Tips

1. **Keep intel current** - Competitors change fast; review quarterly
2. **Track win/loss themes** - Pattern recognition beats anecdotes
3. **Use real customer language** - Quote actual feedback from deals
4. **Don't just list features** - Focus on why customers choose them
5. **Include indirect competitors** - Spreadsheets and manual processes compete too
6. **Document evidence** - Link themes to specific deals or customer quotes

## Gathering Competitive Intel

- **Win/loss interviews** - Talk to customers after they choose (or don't choose) you
- **Sales team feedback** - They hear objections and comparisons daily
- **G2/Capterra reviews** - Public customer feedback on competitors
- **Competitor websites** - Positioning, pricing (when public), messaging
- **Demo their product** - Sign up for trials and experience it
- **Industry analysts** - Gartner, Forrester reports on your category
