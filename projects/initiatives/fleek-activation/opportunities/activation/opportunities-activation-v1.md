## Assessment (Parent-Level Comparison)

### Parent A: Trust & Legitimacy Barriers (Opps 1-6)

**Opportunities:**
- Opp 1: Blurry graphics make platform look like scam
- Opp 2: Can't figure out supplier ratings  
- Opp 3: Quality control invisible
- Opp 4: Bad reviews shown first
- Opp 5: Buyer protection hidden
- Opp 6: Email quality inconsistent

**Factors Considered:**

**Opportunity Sizing:**
- 4 of 5 user feedback themes relate to trust (most frequent concern)
- Affects users at EVERY touchpoint: signup, supplier pages, emails
- Universal barrier (all personas affected)
- UX audit marks 3 as **CRITICAL**

**Market Factors:**
- Table stakes for marketplace trust (Vinted, Depop solved this)
- Competitors show buyer protection prominently
- Trust is prerequisite for B2B transactions

**Company Factors:**
- **Strategic Alignment:** Activation is Hannah's stated priority
- **Resource Considerations:** Opps 1, 5, 6 are quick wins (design/visibility)
- **Existing Assets:** Buyer protection exists but hidden, QC exists but not communicated
- **Quick Wins:** Opp 1 (graphics), Opp 5 (move buyer protection), Opp 6 (email templates)

**Customer Factors:**
- High importance (quoted 7 times across trust themes)
- Very low satisfaction (users need human validation: "spoke to someone physically")
- Existing alternatives: Established marketplaces have trust solved

**Discussion:**
Trust is foundational - users won't engage if platform looks like scam. **Multiple quick wins available** that could compound: fix graphics + surface buyer protection + improve review sorting = measurable trust lift.

**Tentative Winners (3 Quick Wins):** 
- Opp 1 (Blurry graphics) - Most universal, design refresh
- Opp 5 (Buyer protection hidden) - Move to prominent location
- Opp 4 (Bad reviews first) - Adjust algorithm
